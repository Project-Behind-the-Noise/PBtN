About the Students

Ari Tajonar - Founder, Writer, and Interviewer

I’m Ari Tajonar, a high school Sophomore, aspiring music journalist, and the founder of Project Behind the Noise. Music has always been a huge part of my life, especially alternative genres like emo, shoe-gaze, and nu-metal. I’ve always been drawn to the stories behind the music–the passion, the struggles, and the creative process that goes into every song. When I’m not writing or interviewing artists, I’m probably deep-diving into new bands, writing stories, or brainstorming new projects that peak my interests. I’m just a high school student trying to make a name for myself in the journalism world–and I’m excited to see where this journey takes me.

Max Castro - Photographer for both PBTN and The Journal at SHS and Editor for PBTN

I’m Max Castro, a Sophomore in high school, a photographer and a reader. I love the arts, whether it be through pencil and paper, through the natural world or through a camera lens. I’ve always had a passion for photography since I was thirteen. Music especially has been a big part of my life ever since I was little. I love indie music, I listen to that the most, but other alternative music genres have been making a massive breakthrough in my playlists recently. Journalism is also another thing I love, being a part of my school's journal has been even more amazing and helped me follow through with my dreams.

Edgar Bales - Social Media Manager and Content Curator

I’m Edgar Bales and I’m also a Sophomore in high school. I enjoy reading and researching anything of personal interest, like forensics or bands I personally enjoy. Music has also been a huge part of my life growing up. Growing up during the emo phases of my siblings and my mother, I’ve grown to love that form of music. Alternative music has just grown up with me through multiple ways and even now where I get to listen and find so much more styles and artists to listen to. I also enjoy coding and being able to code for this project is actually extremely helpful for helping me get better.
